# CarryForge: Structural Design Brainstorm

## The Core Problem

Current structure:
- 12 quarters, hard stop
- Single MOIC goal (pass/fail)
- Bad luck in Q1 = game is already over emotionally
- Good player in Q8 has no reason to keep going
- "I lost by 0.1x" feels terrible
- "I got 2.4x and wanted to keep building" is a common frustration

---

## Option A: Infinite Fund — You Decide When to Wind Up

**How it works:**
- No timer. The game runs as long as you want.
- A "Wind Up Fund" button ends the game and shows your final score.
- Score = your MOIC + a "legacy rating" based on how long you held on (more exits = richer narrative).
- The game creates narrative pressure instead of a countdown: LP satisfaction decays, deals get harder, rivals get stronger.

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah, 5 min on phone):*
Buys 2 companies, exits one at 1.8x, feels great, hits "Wind Up" whenever she wants. No frustration. Comes back for another run. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus, wants to optimize):*
Pushes to maximize MOIC before winding up. Tries to time the perfect exit window. Plays 3+ hours in one sitting. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
Keeps playing to see what events come next. Never wants to stop because something dramatic might happen. LP drama is the reward. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Appreciates the open-endedness. Tries to hold to 5+ year IRR sweet spots. Understands that real PE funds run 10 years. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Confused — "when do I win?" No clear finish line. Might just close the tab. ⚠️

**Verdict:** Great for engagement and replayability. Needs a strong pull to wind up (an obvious "you're peaking" moment). First-timers need some guidance.

## Option B: Prestige System (Fund I → Fund II → Fund III)

**How it works:**
- Fund I: $50M, 12 quarters, max 4 companies. Normal game.
- At Q12, instead of "you won/lost," you see: "Fund I Complete. Final MOIC: 1.8x."
- Performance determines what you UNLOCK for Fund II:
  - 1.0x: Fund II starts with same $50M
  - 1.5x: Fund II starts with $75M + 1 new sector unlocked
  - 2.0x: Fund II starts with $100M + Fund II-exclusive deals
  - 2.5x+: Fund III tier unlocked eventually
- Each fund adds mechanics (rivals compete, LP types expand, market cycles)
- Carries narrative continuity: your rival from Fund I remembers you

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Finishes Fund I, gets a "B" grade, sees what unlocked. Excited to do Fund II. Clear sense of progress. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Obsessed with getting max unlocks. Restarts to optimize Fund I. Natural progression loop. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
Loves that the rival "remembers" her. Narrative continuity across funds. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Realistic arc: start small, scale. Validates PE career progression. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Clear win condition still exists (finish the fund). Unlock screen provides positive reinforcement even on mediocre performance. ✅

*Replayability chaser (Jake):*
Every fund feels different. Higher-tier funds have harder mechanics. Plays for hours. ✅

**Verdict:** Strongest option for retention and progression feel. Solves win/lose by replacing it with "how much do you unlock." Every run feels meaningful.

## Option C: LP-Driven Soft Deadline

**How it works:**
- No hard 12-quarter cap.
- Instead, your LP satisfaction is the only real pressure.
- LPs "call the fund" when satisfaction hits 0 — game ends.
- You can choose to extend by making smart moves.
- A "Voluntary Wind-Up" button available whenever.
- Effectively: bad play shortens the game, good play extends it.

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Makes some bad choices, LPs pull out in Q8. "Oh, I see — that's why I shouldn't ignore the LP calls." Natural game-over with clear causality. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Tries to keep LPs happy indefinitely. Discovers it gets harder each quarter. Natural escalation. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
LP events feel genuinely urgent now. "I can't ignore this call or the game ends." Choices matter. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Realistic pressure. Real funds die when LP relationships collapse. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
"Why did the game end?" Not enough feedback. Confusing LP system may frustrate. ⚠️

*Aggressive/reckless player (Tyler):*
Ignores LPs, game ends in Q5. Might feel unfair on first playthrough. ⚠️

**Verdict:** Creates the most authentic drama. Risk: first-timers confused by invisible timer. Pair with clear LP bar and warnings.

## Option D: Multiple Win Paths (Parallel Goals)

**How it works:**
- 12 quarters still exists, but three independent goals:
  - 💰 **Returns King**: Hit 2.0x+ MOIC → "you crushed it financially"
  - 🤝 **LP Legend**: Keep satisfaction ≥80 the whole game → "they'll fund you forever"
  - 🏆 **Exit Machine**: Close 5+ exits → "deal maker of the year"
- At the end, you see which you achieved. Any one = "victory."
- All three = "S-tier."
- None = still shows what happened (never just "you lost")

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Gets "Exit Machine" by just selling a lot. Feels rewarded without needing to understand MOIC math. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Goes for all three simultaneously. Clear challenge. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
Prioritizes LP Legend because the relationship narrative is more interesting. Different playstyle. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Chooses Returns King naturally. Validates their instincts. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Three goals is slightly overwhelming but they'll naturally fall into one path. OK. ⚠️

*Speedrunner (Jake):*
"Exit Machine" run = buy fast, sell fast, ignore returns. New playstyle emerges. ✅

**Verdict:** Excellent for replayability and different play styles. Each path becomes its own meta-game.

## Option E: Episodic / One Deal at a Time

**How it works:**
- Each "episode" = one deal tracked from offer to exit.
- No portfolio management. Just one company at a time.
- Short session: source → negotiate → operate → exit. ~10 minutes.
- Score per deal. Cumulative score across episodes.
- Events happen per-deal. More character depth per company.
- Between deals: choose next deal from 3 options.

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Perfect for 10-minute sessions. Each deal is complete in itself. Dopamine loop is tight. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Wants to manage a portfolio simultaneously. Feels too limited. ❌

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
Loves the per-company narrative depth. Each CEO gets more screen time. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Finds it unrealistic — real PE is portfolio management, not sequential. ❌

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Easiest to learn. One thing at a time. Great onboarding. ✅

**Verdict:** Great for mobile casual play but loses the portfolio strategy depth that makes the game interesting.

## Option F: Living World (No End State)

**How it works:**
- Game never ends. You keep raising funds, growing AUM, hiring partners.
- More like Kairosoft / Progress Quest.
- Goal: grow from $50M Fund I to a $1B+ mega fund.
- Milestones: hit $100M AUM, then $250M, then $500M, etc.
- Rivals try to outgrow you. Market cycles matter.
- Retire when you want to.

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Opens the app to check on her fund. Plays in 5-min bursts. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Wants leaderboard. "Who has the biggest AUM?" Natural competitive layer. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
Watches the narrative unfold over weeks. Long-form storytelling. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Appreciates the long-arc strategy. Feels like running an actual firm. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Overwhelmed with no clear endpoint. ❌

**Verdict:** Best long-term engagement but heavy to build. V2 territory.

## Option G: Fixed Seasons / Market Cycles

**How it works:**
- 12 quarters still exists but is divided into **3 seasons** of 4 quarters each:
  - Season 1 (Bull): high multiples, easy exits, build your portfolio
  - Season 2 (Transition): volatility, some companies struggle, LP pressure
  - Season 3 (Bear/Reckoning): multiples compressed, forced decisions, exits harder
- Score is shown per-season AND final.
- The dramatic arc is built in: rise, drama, climax.
- If you exit all companies before Season 3 starts = "tactful timing" bonus.

**Player simulations:**

*Casual (Sarah):*
Season 1 feels approachable. Dramatic shift in Season 2 creates surprise. ✅

*Competitive (Marcus):*
Obsesses over timing exits before the bear market. Skill expression. ✅

*Story-seeker (Priya):*
The market cycle IS the narrative. Feels like a real story arc. ✅

*Finance professional (David):*
Realistic — PE is about timing cycles. ✅

*First-timer (Zoe):*
Clear phases give structure. "Now I'm in the hard part" is obvious. ✅

**Verdict:** Elegant. Preserves fixed length but adds dramatic arc. Pairs beautifully with Option B (prestige) or D (multiple goals).

---

## Recommended Combination

### **"Prestige + Seasons + Multiple Win Paths"**

```
Fund I ($50M, 12 qtrs, 3 seasons)
  ↓
Score = MOIC + LP satisfaction + exits
  ↓
Unlocks for Fund II based on score
  ↓
Fund II has harder mechanics, more capital
  ↓
Repeat until Fund III (endgame)
```

And instead of "you won/lost" → show **3 achievement tracks** at the end:
- 💰 Returns King
- 🤝 LP Legend  
- 🏆 Deal Maker

Any one achieved = satisfying. All three = brag-worthy.

## Other Non-Structural Issues Found

1. **Buy button is too binary** — should have "counter-offer" option (negotiation mini-game)
2. **Selling feels abrupt** — should show a brief "exit recap" modal before confirming
3. **Deal flow cards all feel the same** — need 1-2 sentence "pitch" on each deal
4. **No sense of the rival's arc** — rival should appear in events and on score screen
5. **First quarter has no tutorial moment** — first-time player confusion
6. **LP satisfaction decaying invisibly** — need explicit warning before crisis, not at crisis
7. **"Next Quarter" is low-stakes** — should feel like a page turn, not a form submit
8. **Score screen is too final** — should tease "what's next" (Fund II unlock) even on loss
